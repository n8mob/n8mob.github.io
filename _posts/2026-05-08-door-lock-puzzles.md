If you've heard me go on and on about MAGiE, you may remember that after implementing the basic game I got stumped for puzzle content. So, I drafted a story about Proti and Hepi, two young teenagers who live in an apartment complex connected to a mall and also to their school.

    One day, Proti and Hepi were at the mall, a very exciting series of events led to them getting captured by robots and thrown into Mall Jail.

    The bots drive them each into a cell and stop. Proti feels the bot grip her left arm more tightly, but it releases the right one. The robot is doing *something*. Interfacing with a box mounted to the bars next to the door. The cell doors slide shut **between** the girls and the bots. Proti in her cell and Hepi in her cell, and their bots that are now *outside* the cells, but still holding on to them.

    The doors give a familiar buzz and click, but instead of reminding them of letting friends into their apartments, it gives them a locked-in feeling. After a pause, and just before panic sets in, the security bots release their arms and withdraw.

    "What the crap!?" they both say in unison.

---

And so, I implemented Door Lock puzzles. My first real, new puzzle type in 6 years!!!

The puzzle I implemented doesn't match the door locks in the Mall Story exactly. But the story and the game are both works in progress, so, we'll be alright!
